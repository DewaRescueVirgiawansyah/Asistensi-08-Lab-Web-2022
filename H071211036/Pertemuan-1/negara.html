    fun facts about France

    1. France Has The Largest Art Museum

      2. The Louvre Museum in Paris, France is the largest art museum in the world.
      This museum is so big that if you wanted to spend 30 seconds looking at each piece
      of art, it would take you approximately 100 days to get through the entire museum.


    2. France Produces Over 1,500 Types Of Cheese

      3. In fact, France ranks in the top ten countries for their amount of cheese consumption.
      More than 95% of the French population eats cheese. French cheeses have several defining
      characteristics such as how long they age, the process of making the cheese, and how moldy they are.


    3. French was the official language of England for about 300 years
      4. French was the official language of England between 1066 and 1362.
      Aafter William the Conqueror led the Norman conquest and subsequent occupation
      of England in 1066, he introduced Anglo-Norman French to the nation.


    4. France was the first country in the world to ban supermarkets from throwing away food
      5. In February 2016, France became the first country in the world to ban supermarkets
      from throwing away or destroying unsold food. Stores must now donate surplus groceries
      to food banks and charities.


    5. Europes highest mountain is Mont Blanc in the French Alps

      6. Standing at a height of 4,807m, Mont Blanc is officially the second-
      highest mountain in Europe.
